Lassa fever is an illness caused by Lassa virus. Lassa fever virus is mainly a disease that is animal-borne or spread to humans from animals. People most often become infected by inhaling air contaminated with droppings or urine of infested rats, swallowing the virus in food or contaminated utensils and eating rats or non-domesticated animals called bush meat. Lassa fever is believed to be always present in Ghana, Sierra Leone, Liberia and Nigeria. The incubation period for lassa fever is from six days to three weeks. Most people have mild or no symptoms. In those who have symptoms, it begins with flulike illnesses like fever, generalized weakness, sore throat, severe headache, chest pain, nausea,, vomiting, abdominal pain and diarrhea. How do we then prevent these deadly diseases? The most important preventive methods against lassa outbreak is to eliminate rodent habitats by keeping our environments dean, covering up our food properly, and having access to clean water.
