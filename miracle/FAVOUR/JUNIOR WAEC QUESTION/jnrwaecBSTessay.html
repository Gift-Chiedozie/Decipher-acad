c.

State three differences between conduction and radiation.

i. In conduction, heat transfer is by direct physical contact. While in radiation, heat transfer occurs through electromagnetic waves that do not require physical contact.

ii. Conduction is a relatively slower mode of heat transfer compared to radiation. The transfer of heat occurs progressively from molecule to molecule within a substance. Radiation is a faster mode of heat transfer compared to conduction. Electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light, allowing heat to be transferred over large distances quickly.

iii. Conduction requires a physical medium, such as a solid, for the transfer of heat. It cannot occur in a vacuum. Radiation can occur in a vacuum, it does not require a material medium for propagation.

Define tools.

Tools are objects or devices that are specifically designed to perform a particular task or achieve a specific purpose.
b.

List the types of tools we have and briefly explain them with examples

1. Hand Tools: These are manual tools that are operated by hand without the need for external power sources. Examples of hand tools include hammers, screwdrivers, wrenches, pliers, chisels, hand saws, files, and measuring tools like rulers and tape measures.

2. Power Tools: These are tools that are powered by electricity, batteries, or compressed air. Examples include drills, circular saws, electric sanders, grinders, routers, nail guns, impact wrenches, and power screwdrivers.

c.

State FIVE safety tips when using workshop tools and machine.

1. Inspect tools and machines before use to make sure they are in good condition. 2. Read the operator’s manual before using any power tool. 3. Use tools and machines for their intended purpose. 4. Use gloves, eye protection, and other Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as needed. Examples of PPE include earplugs or earmuffs, goggles, coverall, nose masks, safety shoes, helmet etc. 5. Maintain focus and avoid distractions. 6. Maintain a clean and organized workspace. Keep your work area clean and tidy to avoid trip hazards. 7. Discard any worn out-tools such as hammers, saws, etc. 8. Use proper techniques and grips. Use clamps or another type of stability device to secure your work piece when using handheld power tools. 9. Avoid wearing loose clothing or jewelry that could get caught in moving parts. 10. Machines should be switched off & unplugged after every use and when not in use. 11. Be sure to use the proper tool for the job at hand. 12. Ensure that the work area is dry and clean when using electrical tools. 13. Check for any loose screws or equipment as well as wom-out cords, as they may cause accidents and electrocution. 14. Tools not in use should be kept at the appropriate place. 15. The workshop should be well lit for clear view of the machines.
