In each of the following sentences, there is one underlined word and one gap. From the list of words lettered A to D, choose the one that is most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word and that will at the same time, correctly fill the gap in the sentence.

While Mr. Ade is……, his son is a novice.

A. enlightened
B. competent
C. clever
D. experienced
The correct answer is D. experienced.

The word “experienced” is the most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word “novice.” While a novice lacks experience and is new to a particular field or skill, someone who is experienced has accumulated knowledge and skill through practice and familiarity. In this context, Mr. Ade is experienced, while his son is a novice.
