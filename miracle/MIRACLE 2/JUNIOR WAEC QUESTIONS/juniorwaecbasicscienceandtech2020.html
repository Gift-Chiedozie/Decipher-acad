A. Circular saw

C. Thicknesses
D. Wood lathe
The correct answer is Option A. Aluminum



Aluminum is a metal that remains solid under normal conditions, including room temperature.

On the other hand,

Alcohol is a liquid at room temperature. It does not have a fixed shape but takes the shape of its container, which is a characteristic of liquids, not solids.

Also,
Kerosene is a type of hydrocarbon that is commonly used as a fuel. At room temperature and pressure, kerosene is a liquid, not a solid.
And lastly,
Petroleum is a naturally occurring liquid found in rock formations. It consists of a complex mixture of hydrocarbons. Like alcohol and kerosene, petroleum is a liquid at room temperature.
